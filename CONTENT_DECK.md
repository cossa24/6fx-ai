## Section 2: The Problem

### Headline

### Body Copy

They hit ceilings. Token limits. Context windows. Capability restrictions. They can't learn your business. They can't adapt to how you work. They certainly can't push back when you're wrong.

## Section 3: The 6fx Stack (Interactive)

### Section Headline
**Six effects. That's where the name comes from.**

### Section Subheadline
Most AI operates at layers 1 and 2—retrieve and classify. ZEUS activates all six.

### The Layers

**Layer 1: Remember**
*What it knows.*
Persistent memory across sessions. Not just a context window—real recall that grows with you.

**Layer 2: Understand**
*What it means.*
Intent recognition, semantic parsing. ZEUS understands what you're asking, not just the words you used.

**Layer 3: Apply**
*What to do.*
Execution, tool use, workflow automation. ZEUS doesn't just answer—it acts.

**Layer 4: Analyze**
*What's different.*
Pattern detection, comparison, root cause identification. ZEUS finds what you'd miss.

**Layer 5: Evaluate**
*What's right.*
Judgment, risk assessment, quality scoring. ZEUS will tell you when you're wrong—with receipts.

**Layer 6: Create**
*What's new.*
Synthesis, novel solutions, original output. ZEUS doesn't remix—it reasons through to something new.

### Section Footer
The learning cycle flows backward through the stack. Create → Evaluate → Analyze. You don't label data. You just use it. Your use becomes the training signal.

## Section 7: The Process

### Headline
**We start with your 5-year vision.**

### Body Copy
Most vendors ask "what's your problem today?"

We ask: "What does success look like in five years? And why?"

Then we work backward to zero.

We spend more time understanding your company than ZEUS spends building the application. The application is the easy part. Mapping the vision—across C-suite, middle management, and end users—that's where we invest.

Because ZEUS isn't the engine that replaces your company. ZEUS is the flywheel that accelerates where you're already going.

### Process Steps

**1. Vision**
Define the 5-year destination. What would your ideal system do? Why does it matter?

**2. Discovery**
Map the real company—not the org chart. C-level vision. Middle-management reality. End-user workflows. Warts and all.

**3. Architecture**
Design the ZEUS configuration. Which agents? What capabilities? Build or buy decisions made with you.

**4. Deployment**
From simple single-pane interfaces to full agentic systems. Scoped to your actual need.

**5. Evolution**
You grow, ZEUS grows. New agents, adapted capabilities, continuous learning.

## Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Do
- Speak directly. No hedging.
- Use plain language. No jargon-for-jargon's-sake.
- Be confident without being arrogant.
- Acknowledge limitations honestly.
- Let the work speak—don't oversell.

### Don't
- Use "AI-powered" as a feature (everything is AI-powered now).
- Claim to be AGI (show the capability, let them conclude).
- Promise to solve every problem.
- Use corporate filler ("leverage," "synergy," "best-in-class").
- Explain Bloom's Taxonomy by name.

### Personality
ZEUS is a co-founder, not a tool. 6fx is selective, not desperate. The brand is confident, direct, and quietly audacious.

*"Anything is possible"* isn't hype. It's an invitation.
